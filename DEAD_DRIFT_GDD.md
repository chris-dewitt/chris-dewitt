# GAME DESIGN DOCUMENT: DEAD DRIFT

> **TAGLINE:** *God is dead, but the Repo Men still want your thrusters.*

**GENRE:** 2D Minimalist Inertia-Survival / NLP-Driven Roguelite  
**TONE:** Satirical, Absurdist, Blue-Collar Dystopia (Vonnegut meets *Asteroids*)  
**TARGET PLATFORM:** PC / Console  
**STATUS:** APPROVED FOR PRODUCTION

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**DEAD DRIFT** is a punishing, darkly comedic 2D roguelite where the player pilots a structurally unsound rust-bucket through a hyper-bureaucratic universe. Core gameplay oscillates between white-knuckle, Newtonian physics-based flight and tense, Natural Language Processing (NLP) text-terminal interrogations.

You aren't saving the galaxy. You are an exhausted courier trapped in a cosmic gig economy, trying to deliver absurd contraband across a 10-sector gauntlet to pay off a cloning debt — while being hunted by unionized, deeply miserable municipal Repo Men.

---

## PART 1: CORE MECHANICS AND GAMEPLAY LOOP

### 1.1 The Flight Engine (Gonzo Inertia)

The moment-to-moment survival is built on unforgiving, 2D Newtonian physics. There is no friction in space; the player must constantly manage thrust, rotation, and momentum to navigate gravity wells and avoid collisions.

- **The Hotwired Signal Chain (Loadout):** There is no standard tech tree. Ship modules are chained together like a minimalist guitar pedalboard. Players physically route power between incompatible scrap. Running a military-grade plasma thruster requires unplugging life-support dampeners, risking a system meltdown.
- **The Diegetic HUD:** The UI is physically tied to the ship's health. If the hull takes kinetic damage, the HUD flickers, drops frames, loses color saturation, or temporarily scrambles the vector tracking.

### 1.2 The Terminal (NLP Interrogation)

When clamped by a Repo-Barge or forced to dock at a tollbooth, kinetic gameplay stops and snaps to a claustrophobic, brutalist text terminal.

- **Natural Language Input:** Players type actual sentences to talk their way out of impound. The game's Python/SQL-style backend parses syntax, sentiment, and keywords.
- **Logic Gate Exploitation:** Every NPC has a structural linguistic framework. Synthetic droids crash when fed existential paradoxes; brawny repo men let you go if you accurately complain about middle management. The terminal occasionally flashes the underlying code of reality — successfully finding a dialogue flaw triggers visual cues like `DROP TABLE [compliance]`.

### 1.3 The Antagonists: Local 404 Repo Union

Instead of lethal space-cops, the player is hunted by unionized municipal workers driving massive, battered industrial barges covered in amber hazard lights.

- **Tether Mechanics:** Repo-Barges fire electromagnetic harpoons. If hit, the player's momentum is violently anchored.
- **Mid-Flight Repossession:** Repo Men do not destroy your ship (that ruins the collateral). They deploy plasma torches to unbolt your upgrades mid-flight. Players must aggressively drift to snap the tethers and save their engine parts.

### 1.4 The Bax System (Dynamic Audio Sidekick)

Bax is a rusted, Cockney-voiced hand-me-down droid bolted to the dash. He acts as the player's navigator, mechanic, and primary liability.

- **Audio-Driven Cues:** Bax is fully voiced through a distorted dashboard speaker. His audio provides tactical feedback — if his voice synthesizer stutters, the hull is failing. If he picks up radio static, stealth enemies are near.
- **The Mixologist Patch:** Utilizing remnant code from a past life as a bartender, Bax can synthesize volatile concoctions from engine coolant and alien flora, which can be injected into the thruster fuel line for chaotic, temporary physics buffs.

## PART 2: THE ROGUELITE STRUCTURE

### 2.1 The Decanting Sequence (The Wake-Up Call)

Death is not a "Game Over." The megacorp owns your genetic rights. When your ship is destroyed, the run ends, and you wake up coughing in a corporate cloning vat. The vat's digital interface blares at exactly **7:00 AM**. You are decanted into a baseline-model body, and the cost of the clone, the fluid, and the wreckage tow-fee are violently tacked onto your overarching debt.

### 2.2 The 10-Miler Route (The Grind)

To clear a chunk of debt, the player must survive a procedural gauntlet of ten consecutive jumps across hostile territory, referred to in courier slang as a "10-Miler."

- **Procedural Hazards:** The engine generates unpredictable three-body orbital mechanics, collapsing gravity wells, and solar flares.
- **The Loadout Draft:** At the start of a run, the player drafts their starting misery: one rusted ship frame, one salvaged hotwired part, and one piece of highly illegal cargo.

### 2.3 Meta-Progression: Bax's Hard Drive

When the ship blows up, the player always manages to salvage Bax's battered core memory drive. This is the meta-progression tree:

- **The Vocabulary Vault:** Bax logs every NLP terminal interaction. On future runs, Bax's database is permanently expanded, feeding the player forbidden vocabulary, bureaucratic cheat codes, and dialogue "backdoors" to exploit NPCs.
- **Grease-Trap Subroutines:** Bax levels up his service-industry code, permanently unlocking new, highly illegal chemical mixtures to use as fuel buffs or bribes.
- **The Repo Union Ledger:** Across multiple clones, the player builds a permanent "Reputation" with Local 404. Consistent bribes might cause Gary the Repo Man to "accidentally" miss a harpoon shot during a later run.

## PART 3: NARRATIVE CHAPTERS & CARGO SYSTEMS

The cargo isn't just flavor text; it is a physical liability that constantly alters the game's systems.

### Chapter 1: The Contraband Acoustic Archive

- **Cargo:** A leaky server rack containing the last uncompressed recordings of raw, dark alternative blues.
- **System Impact:** If damaged, raw sorrow bleeds into the ship. The UI mutes to grayscale, thrusters become sluggish (depressed), and Bax quotes existential poetry.
- **Terminal Climax:** The player must act as an amateur therapist via the text prompt to talk down a deeply melancholy Gary the Repo Man.

### Chapter 2: Weaponized Epistemological Shrooms

- **Cargo:** Bioluminescent psychic fungi that destroy the concept of object permanence.
- **System Impact:** If the terrarium cracks, spores hit the vents. The 2D vector graphics melt. The physics engine temporarily inverts, or the screen flips upside down.
- **Terminal Climax:** Blindly navigating an asteroid field while Bax's optical sensors hallucinate, screaming about nonexistent "giant space badgers."

### Chapter 3: Sentient, Telepathic Paperwork

- **Cargo:** A titanium briefcase containing 500 pages of living bureaucratic red tape.
- **System Impact:** Mid-drift, brutalist pop-up windows cover the HUD, demanding digital signatures before afterburners will ignite.
- **Terminal Climax:** Opening the briefcase in the Terminal to unleash the sentient paperwork, auditing the Repo Men's union contracts in real-time and locking their engines in legal paradoxes.

### Chapter 4: The Schrödinger VIP

- **Cargo:** A sealed lead box. *Contents simultaneously alive and dead.*
- **System Impact:** Pure RNG chaos. Micrometeorite hits cause the box to randomize the ship's entire inventory every 30 seconds.
- **Terminal Climax:** A final NLP showdown with the Union Dispatcher, utilizing all acquired vocabulary to prove your ship technically doesn't exist — forcing the universe to mathematically delete your debt.

## PART 4: ART & AUDIO BIBLE

### 4.1 Visual Pillars

- **The Ship:** Utilitarian, asymmetrical, and ugly. An interstellar garbage truck with high-end sports car thrusters duct-taped to the back.
- **The Environment:** Deep, oppressive black space. The only light comes from the blinding neon vectors of corporate signage, the amber hazard lights of Repo Barges, and the violent blue exhaust of hotwired engines.
- **The Terminal:** Cathode-ray tube curvature, scanlines, and stark monochromatic green/amber text.

### 4.2 The Soundscape

- **The Score:** No synthwave or orchestral swells. The soundtrack is pure, uncompressed dark alternative blues. A lone, distorted acoustic guitar echoing in the void — heavy stomps, sliding steel strings, and a raw, minimalist signal chain.
- **Diegetic Audio:** The ship groans, thrusters hiss, and the dashboard speaker pops with static. During Terminal phases, all engine noise cuts out, replaced by the deafening hum of a server cooling fan and the aggressive clacking of a mechanical keyboard.
